So I had an idea on how you could implement something like this.

Okay, so imagine there are three different colored buttons you need to press.
 On the main console, you see a green light with a circle symbol on it, 
 a blue light with a stick and a ball shape on it, and a yellow light with a 
 cross symbol under it. These lights are lit up, but above each one, there's a 
 red light that isn't lit. That red light will turn on when the player finds 
 and flips the matching switch somewhere in the level. That way, you can 
 easily see which ones you've already done.

Now here's the fun—or tricky—part. The switches you need to find are hidden
 cleverly around the level. One is a lever, one is a button, and the other
  is a wheel you turn (like a pressure release valve).

Because the game has fixed camera angles and you can't really look around 
freely, the player has to carefully examine each scene to find what they 
need. I'm guessing that's why they have a flashlight—to help reveal
 certain things in the environment. (Side note: maybe the flashlight 
 could also act as a tool to reveal things you wouldn't normally see 
 without it, or even help activate something.)
Anyway, here's an example: let's say for the green button you need to 
press, you don't actually see the button itself in the scene. Instead,
 you see a green glow. You have to figure out that you need to stand 
 in front of it and press it without ever seeing it's actually a button. 
 
 The only clue that the button is in that room might be a sign or a poster 
 with a green circle on it. Or maybe it's more subtle—like a green sphere 
 sitting on a table in the room. It gives you a hint that you're supposed 
 to be in that room.

You could even take it a step further by using that same green color on monsters
 or somewhere else in the level to fake the player out. Maybe they see a glowing 
 piece of nuclear waste that looks like it could be the green button, but it 
 isn't. You could do the same with the valve—have things that are cross-shaped
  to trick the player, or hide the actual valve in a spot where it blends in
   with other cross-shaped objects.

And like you said, we could have symbols in the screens in the room also as 
clues that what you're looking for is in that room. So the rooms that actually
 have the real lever, button, or valve could have computer screens showing the 
 matching symbol. Maybe even the TV head girl monster could be displaying those
  symbols on her screen. I was also thinking that TV head girl could be a less 
  hostile character, and instead the player could interact with her to get clues.
   That could be a cool way to guide them without making it too obvious.
